BMI

Body Mass Index (BMI) is a numerical measure of a person's weight in relation to their height. It provides a general indication of body fat and is widely used as a screening tool for assessing whether an individual is underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obese.

BMR

Basal Metabolic Rate (BMR) is the amount of energy expended by the body at rest to maintain basic physiological functions such as breathing, circulation, and cell production. It represents the minimum calories needed to sustain life and is influenced by factors like age, gender, body composition, and genetics.

MAINTAINANCE CALORIES

Maintenance calories refer to the amount of energy an individual needs to consume in a day to maintain their current body weight. This level of calorie intake is balanced with the energy expended through daily activities and basal metabolic rate (BMR). When a person consumes the same number of calories as their maintenance level, their weight tends to remain stable over time.

ACTIVITY LEVELS

A person's activity level refers to the amount of physical movement and exercise they engage in on a daily basis. It can vary widely from sedentary, involving minimal physical activity, to highly active, incorporating regular vigorous exercise.

RFM

Relative Fat Mass (RFM) is a simple formula for the estimation of overweight or obesity in humans that requires only a calculation based on a ratio of height and waist measurements. Its more accurate than the traditional BMI.

INTERMITTENT FASTING

Intermittent fasting is an eating pattern that cycles between periods of eating and fasting. It doesn't prescribe specific foods but focuses on when to eat. Common methods include the 16/8 method (16 hours of fasting, 8 hours of eating) and the 5:2 method (regular eating for 5 days, restricted calorie intake for 2 non-consecutive days).

HIGH CARB DIET

A high-carb diet emphasizes increased carbohydrate intake, often derived from whole grains, fruits, and vegetables. It provides a quick energy source for the body, supporting physical activity and daily functions.

ATKIN'S DIET

The Atkins Diet is a low-carbohydrate eating plan designed to promote weight loss. It emphasizes high protein and fat intake while restricting carbohydrate consumption. The diet induces a state of ketosis, where the body burns fat for energy.

IDEAL WEIGHT

Ideal weight is calculated for a person by taking into consideration a person's height and age. It is the standard weight as per which a person can be judged about being fit,underweight or overweight.

REQUIRED CALORIES

It is the number of calories that a person needs to consume to achieve their desired goal i.e weight gain, weight loss, muscle building , etc. It is calculated by adding or subtracting calories from the person's mainatainance calories as per the requirement.
